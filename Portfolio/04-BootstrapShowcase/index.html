Fun Facts

The famous "I am your father" line was a secret: To avoid leaks, the script for *The Empire Strikes Back* said that Darth Vader would tell Luke, "Obi-Wan killed your father." Only a few people, including Mark Hamill, knew the real line.

Indiana Jones was almost played by Tom Selleck! Harrison Ford wasn't the first choice for the role. Tom Selleck had to drop out due to scheduling conflicts with *Magnum, P.I.*, which led Ford to take on the iconic role.

Link’s name has a deeper meaning: The name "Link" was chosen because the character was meant to be a “link” between the player and the game world. Originally, the game was going to involve time travel, and Link was also intended to be a “link” between different eras.

Did you know? Super Mario was originally known as “Jumpman” in *Donkey Kong* before he became Nintendo’s mascot!

The voice of Darth Vader and Mufasa is the same: James Earl Jones provided the iconic voices for both Darth Vader in *Star Wars* and Mufasa in *The Lion King*.

The original *Ghostbusters* theme song was a last-minute addition: Ray Parker Jr. was given only a few days to write the now-famous theme song after other musicians turned down the project.

In the *Harry Potter* movies, Tom Felton (Draco Malfoy) originally auditioned for the roles of both Harry Potter and Ron Weasley before being cast as the villainous Draco.

*Friends* was almost called *Insomnia Café*: Before settling on *Friends*, the creators considered several other titles, including *Six of One* and *Friends Like Us*.

The *Breaking Bad* logo is a clever nod to chemistry: The Br (for Bromine) and Ba (for Barium) in the title sequence reference elements from the periodic table.

The Beatles wanted to make a *Lord of the Rings* movie: They even approached Stanley Kubrick to direct, but J.R.R. Tolkien refused to sell them the film rights.

Crab

Crabs are crustaceans with a hard exoskeleton, called a carapace, which protects their body. They molt periodically, shedding the old exoskeleton to grow.
Most crabs have two large claws, or pincers, which they use for defense, capturing food, and communication.
Crabs usually have a broad, flattened body with a compact shape, often making them look circular or oval.
Many crab species have eyes on stalks that allow them to survey their environment while keeping most of their body hidden.
Crabs have two sets of antennae that help them sense their surroundings and find food.
Unlike many other animals, crabs typically walk sideways. Their legs are structured to move more easily in a lateral direction, which helps them move quickly in tight spaces or on uneven surfaces.
While many crabs are marine animals living in saltwater, some species are terrestrial and even live in freshwater.
They are commonly found in coastal regions, such as beaches, mangroves, and estuaries, and some even live entirely on land.
Crabs are generally omnivores, meaning they eat both plant and animal matter.
Their diet can include algae, plankton, small fish, detritus, and other organic material.
Many crabs communicate through body language, especially using their claws to signal aggression or attract mates.
Crabs often defend their space and burrows from intruders, especially during mating season.
Crabs like to hide in burrows, rocks, or sand to protect themselves from predators and harsh environmental conditions.
Crabs have unique mating behaviors that vary by species, including intricate dances, claw waving, and even sound production.
Female crabs lay hundreds to thousands of eggs, which they often carry in a special abdominal pouch until they’re ready to hatch.
